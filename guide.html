Install Basha

This guide will teach you how to create, install and use your own free version of Basha. You'll have your own site which you'll be able to share with your friends and family.
NOTE: We'll be using a free web hosting service called render.com. At the time of this writing, Render has a limititation of 90 days for free service databases. It shouldn't have a huge impact, but there are some workaround steps you'll need to take. Make sure you read the section on Importing and Exporting.
1
Sign up for account on render.com. This will be where you will host your Basha instance online
2
Once you've got your account set up, simply follow this link. You may be asked to enter credit card info. Don't worry, you won't be charged. Render.com simply uses this to prevent fraud.

You should see a screen like below. For the "service group" name, put whatever you like. You'll need it later, but it doesn't matter too much. Hit the apply button.

3
Creating the app may take a while, anywhere from 10 - 30 minutes. Wait a while, and then go to your dashboard. You should see a table with two items like shown below. Wait until both of the line items' status is green before moving to the next step.Click into the top item which has the type "Web Service." On the next page, you should see a link to your custom Basha site in the title. Follow the link, and you should see a welcome page like this:It may take up to 30 seconds the first time for the screen to show up. Keep retrying until you see it.

Creating a List

1
Once you create your account, the first step will be to create a sentence list. Click on the button "Add a new Deck"
2
You'll be uploading a plain text file where each line is a sentence that your translator will translate and record audio for. You can either build your old text file, or use some one of our curated lists here. Enter the name of your list and the language name.
3
Enter the name of your list and the language name. If your language doesn't use a latin-based writing system, your translator will have the option to submit both a phonetic transation and optionally a translation in the language's native script.
4
Once you submit, you'll be taken back to your dashboard and you will see your created sentence list.

Sharing your List

1
Now, the next step is to share your list. Click on the 'edit' link to be taken to the main list list page. This is the main page that your translator will be working from. When they click "edit" on each sentence, they will be able to easily write a translation and record audio.
2
On this page, click the 'Generate Sharing Link' button. This will give you a URL you can copy and share with any people you are recruiting to translate.When they open the link, they will be able to create an account and start translating your list!

Exporting your List for Learning

1
Be sure to sign in periodically and check on the progess of your translators. When they are finished you'll be able to download an Anki Deck of the the full list from your Dashboard. Simply click the 'Download' link and you'll get a .apkg file you can open with Anki.
2
You can download Anki here. Anki is a a free and open source software that let's you study decks of flashcards. It uses SRS Learning to show you cards you find easy less often, and cards you find difficult more often, taking a lot brainwork out of studying. This is the primary method of learning languages used by Speechling and Glossika.
3
Your Ankideck will contain cards where the front will show the english phrase, and the back will show the translation with autio. If you create a free account with AnkiWeb, you can sync your progress between your phone and your computer. Be sure to publicly share your Anki decks as well. Happy Learning!

Backing Up and Restoring your Basha Site

Unfortunately, while render.com allows us to create our site for free and share with our friends and family, one big drawback is that we can only use our site's database for 90 days. Luckily, we've built in a feature here to help you keep using Basha as long as you need. We recommend doing all of the following steps in one sitting when you get close to the 90 day limit.
1
When you are 3 weeks away from your 90 day limit, you will see a warning banner on the site letting you know that you need to reset your Basha database. You can follow that link or simply go to https://your-basha-site.onrender.com/#/backup at any time.
2
Click the Download Button here and you will get an archive of all the data on your site, including all users, lists, translations and audio. Make sure you immediately do the next steps after this one. All work done on site from now on will be lost until you restore this archive.
3
Go to your render dashboard. You'll see two items in the main table. Click on the second one, which should have the type "PostgreSQL." Scroll all the way to the bottom of the next page, and click "Delete Database"
4
Once the database is deleted, you will be taken back to your dashboard where you will only see one item in your table. Since we deleted the database, your site will not be functioning properly if others are using it. From here, click on the "Blueprints" tab at the top of the site. You should see one entry in the resulting table. I will have the name you chose when you first installed Basha. Click into that item.On the next page, you'll see a button that says "Manual Sync." Click that, and then click "Approve."
5
After a few minutes and then go back to your Dashboard. You'll see that you now have two services again. If they both have a status of "Available" then you can move on to step 6. If you see one with "Service failed," you will need to do one more thing.Click into the failed service, then click on "Manual Deploy" and "Deploy latest commit." Go back to your Dashboard after that, and after 15 minutes or so you should see both services available again.
6
Once your app is redeployed and working, go your website. Since we deleted the database, it will look like a fresh install, and you'll be prompted to make an account. This time instead of making an account, click on the link to "restore an archived Basha site." This will take you to a page where you can upload the archived file you created in step 2. Once you restore your archived site, you will be taken back to the login page and all your old accounts and data will be back.
